Akamai Internship | 2014
Over the summer in 2014 I interned at Akamai http://www.akamai.com/ working on data visualiztion. I was particularly interested in this internship because I already had experience in data visualization from my Game Lab project and was hoping to get some experience in it in terms of web dev. My internship consisted of working in a team of 4 designing and programming visualizations for the 28 screens in Akamai's NOCC http://www.akamai.com/html/technology/nocc.html . My dataset for this was Akamai's maintenance data on their servers in terms of software, maintenance fixes, ongoing problems, etc. One of my main endeavors was creating a geographical representation of all of this data. I did this using Leaflet and D3
Why Leaflet?
Leaflet was Open-source: this was quite useful because I had to go source diving more than once in order to solve a problem with the legacy browser on the NOCC Easy to use tilesets you want: Compatibility with Chloropleths: it's a damn tutorial http://leafletjs.com/examples/choropleth.html
Chloropleth Map
Made a new metric Used a chloropleth because heatmaps inevitably become population maps a la xkcd divided by total capacity because in order to normalize and make sure that when a country was losing a lot of capacity to a ticket, it was shown as a big problem
